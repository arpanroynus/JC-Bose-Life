A 200-Year Legacy of Christmas in the City of Joy

The Evolution of the Festival

1800s

The British Aristocracy

Christmas was a formal affair for the East India Company elite. Grand balls were held at the Government House (now Raj Bhavan), and the air was filled with the sound of cannons and church bells.

Click to see a fun fact!
1902

Nahoum & Sons Opens

The Jewish community in Kolkata adds a new flavor to Christmas. Nahoum's opens in New Market, eventually becoming the 'holy grail' for fruitcakes and brownies.

1940s

Jazz & Park Street

Despite the war years, Park Street emerges as the nightlife hub. The British soldiers and Anglo-Indians turn Kolkata into a city of jazz, shimmering lights, and midnight revelry.

Present Day

The Kolkata Christmas Festival

Now a massive public carnival! Allen Park hosts live choirs, the entire Park Street is lit with blue and white LEDs, and people of all faiths join the queue for cake.

The "Cake Trail"

Flurys

Established 1927. Famous for its "rum-soaked" fruitcakes and English breakfast.

Nahoum's

The 120-year-old Jewish bakery in New Market where the queue starts at 6 AM.

Saldanha

A Goan-run treasure hidden in the lanes of central Kolkata, legendary for walnut cakes.
